A common question from Golf DTV applicants is: "What documents do I need that are different from a regular DTV?" The short answer is that one central document differs. This article covers the Golf DTV document requirements, with a focus on the Acceptance Letter.

---

## Documents Required for All DTV Applications

The following documents are required regardless of which DTV route you use:

| Document | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Passport | Valid for at least 1 year (2+ years recommended), with blank pages available |
| Bank balance certificate | THB 500,000 or more, in English, issued within the last 3 months |
| Bank transaction statements | Last 3 months (increasingly required — prepare in advance from the same account) |
| Passport photo | As specified by the embassy or application portal |
| Application form | Thai e-Visa online form or embassy-specific form |
| Proof of lawful presence | Document showing you are legally present in the embassy's jurisdiction at time of application |

For full details on shared documents, see [DTV Required Documents](/en/blog/dtv-required-documents).

---

## How Golf DTV Documents Differ from Workcation DTV

Here's a comparison of the two main DTV application routes:

| Document Category | Workcation Route | Golf DTV (Soft Power Route) |
|---|---|---|
| Qualifying activity proof | Employment contract or freelance agreement (in English) | Acceptance Letter from a Thai golf facility |
| Remote work authorization | Remote work permission letter, etc. | **Not required** |
| Freelance evidence | English client contracts, invoices, etc. | **Not required** |

**The defining feature of Golf DTV is that employment documents are not required.** In their place, an Acceptance Letter issued by a Thai golf facility serves as the proof of qualifying activity.

---

## The Most Important Golf DTV Document: The Acceptance Letter

### What Is an Acceptance Letter?

The Acceptance Letter is an official document issued by a Thai golf course or facility. It states, in English, that the facility accepts the applicant as someone visiting Thailand for golf activities at their venue.

In the Golf DTV context, the Acceptance Letter plays the same role as an employment contract does in a Workcation application.

### Typical Content of an Acceptance Letter

- Name and address of the facility, including contact information
- Applicant's full name (matching passport)
- Purpose and duration of the visit
- Authorized signature and/or stamp

### How to Obtain an Acceptance Letter

**Option 1: Contact a Thai golf facility directly**
Reach out to a course in English and request an Acceptance Letter. This can work, but language barriers and varying facility policies mean the process is sometimes unpredictable.

**Option 2: Use a Golf DTV support service**
Support services work with facilities that have experience issuing Acceptance Letters, which improves reliability. See the [Golf DTV page](/en/golf-dtv) for details.

## Document Consistency: Key Points for Golf DTV

### Match Your Name Across All Documents

The name on your bank balance certificate, Acceptance Letter, and application form must all match your passport exactly — including spelling and name order. Discrepancies can cause delays during review.

### Use the Same Account for Bank Documents

Your bank balance certificate and transaction statements should come from the same account. Large one-time deposits that appear shortly before the application date tend to stand out in transaction history. Maintaining a stable balance over time is preferable.

### Don't Let the Acceptance Letter Expire

The Acceptance Letter includes its issuance date. Some embassies may flag letters that appear too old. Once you have the letter in hand, submit your application promptly.

## Document Preparation Timeline (Golf DTV Version)

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| 6–8 weeks before travel | Begin Acceptance Letter request (takes the most time) |
| 4–5 weeks before travel | Receive Acceptance Letter; request bank balance certificate |
| 3–4 weeks before travel | Prepare transaction statements; review all documents; submit embassy application |
| 1–2 weeks before travel | Confirm visa issuance |

For the full timeline, see [The Golf DTV Application Process](/en/blog/golf-dtv-process).

## Summary

- Golf DTV requires no employment documents — the Acceptance Letter from a Thai golf facility takes their place
- All other core documents (passport, bank balance certificate, statements) are the same as standard DTV
- Name consistency across all documents and matching bank account records are essential
- The Acceptance Letter can take 2–4 weeks to obtain — start early
- A support service can significantly improve the reliability of Acceptance Letter procurement

For document preparation questions, visit the [Golf DTV page](/en/golf-dtv).
