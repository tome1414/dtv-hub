**One document distinguishes Golf DTV from the Workcation route: the Acceptance Letter.** This English-language letter, issued by a Thai golf facility, replaces the employment contracts and remote work documentation required on the Workcation route. Everything else in the document list is standard DTV.

This article covers what you need, why the Acceptance Letter is the most important single document to get right, and how document consistency determines whether your application moves smoothly.

---

## Documents Required for All DTV Applications

These are required regardless of which route you apply through:

| Document | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Passport | Valid for at least 1 year (2+ years recommended); blank visa pages available |
| Bank balance certificate | THB 500,000+; in English; issued within the last 3 months |
| Bank transaction statements | Last 3 months; from the same account as the balance certificate |
| Passport-style photo | Format specified by the Thai e-Visa portal or embassy |
| Application form | Thai e-Visa online form or embassy-specific form |
| Proof of lawful presence | Document confirming you're legally in the embassy's jurisdiction at time of application |

> **On bank statements:** An increasing number of Thai embassies now request 3 months of transaction history alongside the balance certificate. Prepare both from the same account proactively. A stable balance over time is more credible than a large deposit made just before applying. See [DTV Required Documents](/en/blog/dtv-required-documents) for full details on the standard document set.

## What Golf DTV Requires That Workcation Doesn't

| Document Category | Workcation Route | Golf DTV (Soft Power Route) |
|---|---|---|
| Qualifying activity proof | Employment contract or freelance agreement (in English) | Acceptance Letter from a Thai golf facility |
| Remote work authorization | Required | **Not required** |
| Freelance evidence | Required | **Not required** |

The tradeoff is straightforward: Golf DTV removes the employment documentation burden and replaces it with one document — the Acceptance Letter.

## The Acceptance Letter: What It Is and What It Must Contain

The Acceptance Letter is an official document issued by a Thai golf course or facility. It confirms that the applicant intends to visit the facility for golf activities and that the facility accepts this visit.

### What a Valid Acceptance Letter Must Include

- Full name of the facility, address, and contact information
- Applicant's full name — **exactly as it appears in their passport**
- Purpose of the visit (golf activities)
- Duration or dates of the intended visit
- Authorized signature (and ideally an official stamp)

> **Why each element matters:** If your name on the Acceptance Letter doesn't match your passport exactly — including spelling and name order — it can cause delays or a request to revise the letter. The same applies to vague activity descriptions or missing facility contact details. Embassies use this letter as the primary evidence of your qualifying activity.

### How to Obtain the Acceptance Letter

**Option 1: Contact a Thai golf facility directly**
Reach out to a course in English, explain you need an Acceptance Letter for a DTV Soft Power application, and coordinate the request. This is possible, but outcomes vary significantly by facility — some are experienced with DTV requests, others aren't.

**Option 2: Use a Golf DTV support service**
Specialist services work with facilities that have a track record of issuing DTV-compliant Acceptance Letters. This removes the coordination uncertainty. See [Golf DTV support options](/en/golf-dtv).

> **Timing:** Allow 2–4 weeks from initiating the request to receiving a usable letter. This is the longest single step in the Golf DTV process — start it before preparing any other document.

## Document Consistency: The Most Common Source of Application Problems

The Acceptance Letter and bank documents don't stand alone — they're read together with your passport and application form. Inconsistencies between documents are one of the most common reasons for additional document requests or delays.

### Three Consistency Rules to Follow

**1. Your name must match exactly across all documents.**
The name on your Acceptance Letter, bank balance certificate, and application form must all match the spelling and order of your name as it appears in your passport. Even minor differences — a hyphen, a different order of given and family name — can trigger a request for correction.

**2. Your bank balance certificate and transaction statements must be from the same account.**
Using documents from different accounts makes it harder for the embassy to verify your financial position. Prepare both from the same account.

**3. Don't let the Acceptance Letter sit too long before applying.**
The letter has an issuance date. Some embassies may flag letters that appear very old relative to the application date. Once you have the letter, proceed with the application reasonably promptly.

## FAQ

**Can I use a booking confirmation from a golf course instead of an Acceptance Letter?**
No. A booking confirmation is not equivalent to an Acceptance Letter. The Acceptance Letter is a formal document issued specifically for DTV visa purposes — it has a different format and contains different information.

**What if the facility won't issue an Acceptance Letter?**
Not all Thai golf facilities are familiar with DTV Acceptance Letter requests. If a facility declines or can't issue an appropriate letter, using a support service that has relationships with experienced facilities is a practical alternative.

**Does the Acceptance Letter need to be notarized or apostilled?**
This is not a general requirement for DTV applications, but requirements can vary by embassy. Confirm with your intended embassy if you're uncertain.

**What if my bank account is in a currency other than Thai baht?**
Foreign-currency bank certificates can be used, but the equivalent value in Thai baht must be clearly demonstrable. If the conversion rate isn't stated on the certificate, include a separate note or reference.
