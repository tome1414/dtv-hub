Golf DTV is not for everyone. Some people are a natural fit; others may find it doesn't match their situation. This article assesses suitability across four dimensions: cost tolerance, stay purpose, lifestyle, and document preparedness.

---

## A Quick Refresher on Golf DTV

Golf DTV refers to applying for Thailand's DTV (Destination Thailand Visa) using golf as the qualifying activity under the Soft Power route. The visa is valid for 5 years, allows stays of up to 180 days per entry, and supports multiple entries. See [What Is Golf DTV?](/en/blog/golf-dtv-overview) for a full overview.

---

## Who Golf DTV Tends to Suit

### 1. People who can't easily prepare employment documentation

The DTV Workcation route requires English-language employment evidence — employment contracts, remote work authorization letters, or freelance agreements with overseas clients.

If you are a freelancer without formal contracts, an individual business owner without overseas client agreements, or someone between jobs or taking a sabbatical, Golf DTV's Soft Power route — which requires **no employment documents** — becomes a practical option.

### 2. Those wanting regular, extended stays in Thailand

The DTV's design — up to 180 days per entry, multiple entries over 5 years — suits people who want to spend several months in Thailand annually or rotate between Thailand and their home country on a recurring basis.

Compared to visa-on-arrival (typically 30 days for eligible nationalities), DTV is designed for long-term, repeated use.

### 3. Golf-inclined people over 50 looking for a base in Thailand

Thailand offers affordable golf year-round in a warm climate. For those in retirement or semi-retirement who enjoy golf and want to spend extended time in Thailand, Golf DTV aligns the purpose of the visa with an activity they genuinely plan to do.

Skill level is not a factor. The intent to play golf in Thailand is sufficient.

### 4. People who want professional support for the process

The Acceptance Letter — a document issued by a Thai golf facility — involves coordination from abroad. Those who prefer a reliable, guided process over navigating it independently tend to be well-served by Golf DTV specialist services.

---

## Who Golf DTV May Not Suit

### 1. Those trying to minimize upfront costs

Golf DTV involves several financial commitments:

- Visa application fee: THB 10,000 (waived for some nationalities → [Details](/en/blog/dtv-fee-by-nationality))
- Support service fee (if applicable)
- Financial requirement: THB 500,000 in your bank account at application time

Add to this the cost of flights and living expenses in Thailand. For a full breakdown, see [Golf DTV Costs](/en/blog/golf-dtv-cost).

### 2. Those planning short trips (1–2 weeks)

DTV is built for long-stay, multi-trip use. If you only plan to visit Thailand for a week or two, visa-on-arrival or a tourist visa is likely simpler and more proportionate. The cost and effort of a Golf DTV are justified by frequent or extended use over five years.

### 3. Those whose primary goal is working in Thailand

DTV does not include work authorization in Thailand. Providing services to Thai clients or employers without a Work Permit is not permitted under DTV. Those seeking to live and work in Thailand for local companies should explore employment visas or other appropriate categories.

### 4. Those with very little time before their trip

Obtaining the Acceptance Letter takes 2–4 weeks, and embassy processing takes another 1–3 weeks. If you need to travel imminently, the timeline may not be achievable. Check [The Golf DTV Application Process](/en/blog/golf-dtv-process) for a realistic timeline.

## Common Borderline Cases

| Situation | Golf DTV Compatibility |
|---|---|
| Employed with documented remote work for an overseas employer | Workcation route also worth considering |
| Freelancer without formal overseas client contracts | Golf DTV Soft Power route is a natural fit |
| No interest in golf but wanting long-term stays | Activity basis doesn't align — consider other routes |
| Over 50, planning Thailand retirement life | Retirement visa (Non-OA) also worth comparing |
| Planning to bring family to Thailand | Each family member must apply individually |

---

## Summary

Golf DTV tends to suit:
- Those who can't easily prepare employment documents
- Those who want long-term, recurring access to Thailand over five years
- Golf-inclined people over 50 looking for an extended base in Thailand
- Those who prefer professional support for document preparation

It tends not to suit:
- Short-term tourists trying to minimize costs
- Those whose primary purpose is working for Thai clients or employers
- Those who don't have enough lead time before their travel date

If you're unsure, a free individual consultation is available through the [Golf DTV page](/en/golf-dtv).
