If you want to get a Golf DTV but aren't sure where to start, this article walks through the entire process — from initial planning to what happens after you arrive in Thailand. Understanding the timeline and key steps at each stage will help you prepare smoothly.

---

## Four Stages of the Golf DTV Process

Golf DTV can be broken into four main stages:

| Stage | Description | Approximate Timing |
|---|---|---|
| ① Research & Planning | Understand the program, assess fit, consider support services | 2–3 months before intended travel |
| ② Document Preparation | Obtain Acceptance Letter, prepare bank documents and other materials | 6–8 weeks before intended travel |
| ③ Embassy Application | Apply via Thai e-Visa system or at a Thai embassy or consulate | 3–4 weeks before intended travel |
| ④ After Arrival | Enter Thailand, begin golf activities, manage 180-day stay limit | Ongoing from entry |

---

## Stage ①: Research & Planning (2–3 Months Before Travel)

### What to Confirm First

- **Understand the DTV program**: What is Golf DTV and what kind of visa is it? ([What Is Golf DTV?](/en/blog/golf-dtv-overview))
- **Assess your fit**: Consider whether the costs, stay duration, and lifestyle match your situation ([Golf DTV Suitability](/en/blog/golf-dtv-suitability))
- **Check your bank balance**: Confirm you have at least THB 500,000 (approximately USD 14,000–15,000) in your account

### Considering a Support Service

The Acceptance Letter — issued by a Thai golf facility — is the central document for Golf DTV. Obtaining one involves contacting a Thai facility in English and navigating potential language and procedural barriers from abroad.

Golf DTV specialists can handle everything from Acceptance Letter procurement to document review. See [Golf DTV support details](/en/golf-dtv).

---

## Stage ②: Document Preparation (6–8 Weeks Before Travel)

### Getting the Acceptance Letter (Your Most Time-Sensitive Document)

The Acceptance Letter is an official document issued by a Thai golf course or facility. From the time you make the request to when you receive the letter, **the process can take 2–4 weeks**, so starting early is important.

Steps involved:
- Submit your request to the facility
- The facility issues the letter
- Receive and review the letter

### Other Documents to Prepare

| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| Passport (valid 1+ year) | Check remaining validity and blank pages |
| Bank balance certificate (THB 500,000+, in English) | Banks may take several days to issue |
| Bank transaction statements (last 3 months) | Increasingly required; prepare in advance |
| Passport photo | Match the format specified by the application portal |

For full document details, see [Golf DTV Documents](/en/blog/golf-dtv-documents) and [DTV Required Documents](/en/blog/dtv-required-documents).

## Stage ③: Embassy Application (3–4 Weeks Before Travel)

### How to Apply

DTV applications are submitted at a **Thai embassy or consulate outside Thailand**. Residents of your home country should apply at the embassy with jurisdiction over their area.

Application methods vary by embassy — some accept online submissions through the Thai e-Visa system, while others require in-person submission. Check the specific embassy's latest guidance before applying.

### Key Points at Application

- Document consistency — ensure the name on your passport matches all other documents
- Proof of lawful presence in the embassy's jurisdiction at the time of application
- Visa application fee: THB 10,000 (waived for some nationalities — [see details](/en/blog/dtv-fee-by-nationality))

Processing typically takes **1–3 weeks**, though this varies by embassy and season.

---

## Stage ④: After Arrival in Thailand

### Entry Procedures

DTV holders complete an immigration card and receive an entry stamp at Thai immigration. **Each entry allows a stay of up to 180 days.**

### What to Manage During Your Stay

- **90-Day Report**: If you stay continuously for 90 days or more, you must report to the nearest immigration office
- **Re-entry and extension**: The DTV allows multiple entries over 5 years. To stay beyond 180 days, you must exit and re-enter Thailand
- **Work-related activities**: If you plan to provide services to Thai clients or companies, additional permissions may be required — verify separately

### Starting Golf Activities

Visit the golf facility that issued your Acceptance Letter and enjoy courses across Thailand. It is advisable to maintain genuine golf activities that align with the stated purpose of your visa.

## Summary

- Golf DTV follows four stages: research → document preparation → embassy application → post-arrival
- The Acceptance Letter takes the longest (2–4 weeks), so start early
- Embassy processing takes 1–3 weeks
- After arrival, manage the 90-day report requirement and 180-day stay limit
