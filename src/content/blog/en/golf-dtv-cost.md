One of the most common questions about Golf DTV is: "How much does it actually cost?" This article breaks down costs into four categories. Because exact amounts vary, the goal here is to help you understand how to think about the costs rather than give you a single fixed number.

---

## Four Cost Categories for Golf DTV

| Category | What It Covers |
|---|---|
| ① Visa application fee | The fee paid to the Thai government |
| ② Financial requirement (assets held) | The bank balance required for the application |
| ③ Support service fee | Cost of help obtaining the Acceptance Letter and preparing documents |
| ④ Living costs in Thailand | Accommodation, food, transport, and golf expenses after arrival |

---

## ① Visa Application Fee

The DTV visa application fee is **THB 10,000** (as of May 2026). At current exchange rates, this is roughly USD 280–300, though the equivalent in your home currency will vary.

This fee is charged per application. If you need to reapply after your five-year DTV expires, the fee applies again.

> **Fee waivers by nationality:** Nationals of South Korea, Malaysia, Singapore, and Tunisia are exempt from the application fee under bilateral agreements with Thailand. See [DTV Application Fee by Nationality](/en/blog/dtv-fee-by-nationality) for details.

---

## ② Financial Requirement (Assets Held)

One DTV requirement is having at least **THB 500,000 in a bank account**, documented with an English-language bank balance certificate. This is not a payment — it is an asset requirement.

Approximate equivalent: THB 500,000 ≈ USD 14,000–15,000 (varies by exchange rate)

Your funds are not locked after the application, but they must be demonstrably present **at the time of application**. Embassies increasingly request bank transaction statements for the past three months alongside the balance certificate, so a history of stable balances is more convincing than a large one-time deposit made just before applying.

---

## ③ Support Service Fee

The cost that tends to be unique to Golf DTV applicants is the **support service fee** for obtaining an Acceptance Letter.

The Acceptance Letter is a document issued by a Thai golf facility — the central piece of evidence for a Golf DTV application. Contacting a Thai facility directly from abroad involves language barriers and procedural uncertainty, which is why many applicants use a Golf DTV specialist service.

Fees vary by service provider and plan. Visit the [Golf DTV page](/en/golf-dtv) for service details and current pricing.

---

## ④ Living Costs in Thailand

Costs after arrival depend heavily on your lifestyle and how long you stay. Here are the main cost areas:

### Monthly Living Expenses (Approximate — Varies by Individual)

| Expense | Notes |
|---|---|
| Accommodation | Monthly condo rentals vary widely by location and quality |
| Food | Eating local Thai food tends to cost significantly less than Japan or the West |
| Transport | BTS (Skytrain), MRT, and Grab are inexpensive within Bangkok |
| Health insurance | International health coverage is recommended for extended stays |

### Golf Costs (Approximate)

| Expense | Notes |
|---|---|
| Green fees | Many courses are more affordable than comparable courses in Japan or the US |
| Caddie fees | Caddies are standard at most Thai courses |
| Equipment rental | Available at most courses |

Thailand is widely regarded as offering excellent value for golf — better than most developed countries for comparable quality.

---

## What Makes the Total Cost Vary

Golf DTV costs as a whole are affected by:

- **Exchange rates**: The THB/USD rate affects both the visa fee and financial requirement in your home currency
- **Length of stay**: Longer trips cost more on the ground, but spread the fixed visa cost across more days
- **Location**: Bangkok's central districts are pricier than suburban or provincial areas; hotel vs. condo rental also makes a big difference
- **Support service choice**: Whether you handle preparations independently or use a specialist service

---

## How to Think About Value

**If you plan multiple stays over five years, the cost per trip shrinks**
Once you hold a DTV, you can use it for multiple entries over five years. If you visit Thailand one to two times per year for one to three months each time, the per-trip visa cost becomes modest.

**Thailand's golf and living costs tend to be lower than your home country**
For many visitors from Japan, the US, or Europe, daily life in Thailand costs the same or less than at home, even including golf.

## Summary

- Visa application fee: THB 10,000 (fixed)
- Financial requirement: THB 500,000+ in your bank account (an asset requirement, not a payment)
- Support service fee: varies by provider ([Golf DTV page](/en/golf-dtv) for details)
- Living and golf costs after arrival: lifestyle-dependent, often lower than home country costs
