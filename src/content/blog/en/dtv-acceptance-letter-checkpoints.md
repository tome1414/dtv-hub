For DTV Soft Power route applications, the acceptance letter issued by a Thai facility is the central document. An acceptance letter with insufficient content can weaken the foundation of your application.

**What you'll learn:** What information a well-prepared acceptance letter must include, what to verify, and why letter quality matters.

---

## What Is an Acceptance Letter?

An acceptance letter is a document issued by a Thai facility — a golf course, cooking school, martial arts gym, or similar institution offering Soft Power activities. It serves as a certificate stating that the applicant will participate in activities at that facility.

---

## Information That Should Appear in the Acceptance Letter

A well-prepared acceptance letter clearly contains all of the following:

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Issuing institution information | Facility name, address, contact details, and signature of representative or authorized staff |
| Applicant's name | English spelling matching the passport exactly |
| Type of activity | Specific activity name (golf, cooking, Muay Thai, etc.) |
| Activity period | Start date, end date, or duration |
| Activity format / content | Overview of how participation will take place |
| Issue date | Date the letter was created and issued |

---

## What to Verify

### Is the applicant's name spelled exactly as it appears in the passport?

Confirm that the full name matches the passport format precisely. If there are any spelling errors or differences in order, request a correction.

### Is the activity period clearly stated?

Verify that "from [date] to [date]" or a specific duration is stated. Vague language such as "as needed" or "on an ongoing basis" is best avoided.

### Is the institution information complete?

Confirm that the facility name, address, and signatory are clearly recorded. Letters without a signature, or from institutions with no listed contact information, lack credibility.

### Is the activity described specifically?

Verify that the type of activity is clearly named — for example, "participation in golf program." Vague descriptions like "participation in Thai culture" may require supplemental explanation.

---

## Why Acceptance Letter Quality Matters

The acceptance letter is the foundational document for a Soft Power route application. If this document lacks credibility, additional verification requests or follow-up from the reviewing officer become more likely.

Golf courses and academies experienced with Golf DTV applications tend to produce letters that consistently meet DTV requirements, which reduces the risk of document-related issues. For details, see the [Golf DTV page](/en/golf-dtv).

## FAQ

**Q. Does the acceptance letter need to be in English?**
A. English submission is standard. If the letter is in Thai, an English translation may be required.

**Q. Does the acceptance letter have an expiry date?**
A. A letter issued too far in advance of your application may raise questions about whether the stated activity period is still valid. Having the letter issued shortly before applying, or ensuring the stated period aligns with your application timing, is advisable.

**Q. How do I find a facility that can issue an acceptance letter?**
A. This varies by activity. For golf, check the [Golf DTV page](/en/golf-dtv) for information on facilities experienced with DTV acceptance letters.

**Q. Can I apply with just an acceptance letter?**
A. The acceptance letter is the application basis document for the Soft Power route, but you still need the common required documents — passport, bank balance certificate, etc. Check whether your application embassy's guidance requires any additional documents.
